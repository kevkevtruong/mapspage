Chick-fil-A to stop funding organizations opposed to LGBTQ rights

by Kevin Truong

Chick-fil-A announced late last month it will stop donating to organizations with a history of opposing LGBTQ rights.

In a statement released on its website, the fast food franchise announced it will start focusing its philanthropic efforts specifically in the areas of education, homelessness and hunger. This means a number of previously funded Christian organizations with a history of campaigning against LGBTQ rights will no longer receive funding through the Chick-fil-A Foundation.

Though the LGBTQ community was not specifically mentioned in the announcement, the announcement does come after years of protests by LGBTQ advocates calling for a boycott of Chick-fil-A because of its associations with anti-LGBTQ organizations.

The announcement was met with mixed reactions, with some skeptical of Chick-fil-A's motivations and others uncertain if the fast food franchise will follow through with its pledge.

Mariano Ruiz serves on the board of the International Day Against Homophobia, Transphobia and Biphobia and works in LGBTQ advocacy. He doesn't think Chick-fil-A's announcement will have much impact in the broader fight for LGBTQ rights.

"We need to change the narrative and advocate for change with stronger initiatives and innovative approaches to change hearts and minds," Ruiz said. "Conservatives will always be there."

Whether the announcement will have a positive or negative effect on business for Chick-fil-A has yet to be seen. Of the 21 states with the largest number of Chick-fil-A restaurants in the country, only three have any type of non-discrimination protections for LGBTQ people written into their state law. Those states are California, Colorado and New Jersey.
